🛕 Ambubachi Mela

The Sacred Festival of Divine Feminine Power

🌺 About Ambubachi Mela

Ambubachi Mela is one of the most important religious festivals celebrated at Kamakhya Temple in Assam. Held every year during June, the festival marks the annual menstruation cycle of Goddess Kamakhya and symbolizes fertility, creation, and the divine power of womanhood. It attracts lakhs of devotees, saints, and pilgrims from all over India.

📖 Origin and Belief

According to Hindu tradition, Goddess Kamakhya represents feminine energy (Shakti). During Ambubachi, the Goddess is believed to undergo her annual menstrual cycle. Therefore, the temple remains closed for three days. On the fourth day, after sacred purification rituals, the temple doors reopen for devotees to receive blessings.

✨ Significance

Ambubachi Mela celebrates the creative power of women and honors menstruation as sacred. The festival reflects the unique Tantric traditions of Kamakhya Temple and represents fertility, motherhood, and divine feminine energy.

🌍 Economic & Cultural Impact

Every year, lakhs of pilgrims visit Guwahati during Ambubachi Mela. The festival boosts tourism, supports hotels, transport, local markets, and showcases Assam's rich spiritual and cultural heritage.
