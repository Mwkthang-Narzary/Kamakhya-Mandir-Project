History of Kamakhya Temple

The Kamakhya Temple is one of the oldest and most revered Shakti Peethas in India, situated on the Nilachal Hill in Guwahati, Assam. According to Hindu mythology, the temple marks the place where the yoni (womb) of Goddess Sati fell after the death of the Goddess, making it one of the holiest pilgrimage sites dedicated to Goddess Shakti.

Historical records suggest that the original temple existed long before the 8th century. It was destroyed during invasions and later reconstructed in the 16th century by King Naranarayana and his brother Chilarai of the Koch dynasty. The present temple beautifully combines traditional Hindu architecture with Assamese design, attracting millions of devotees and tourists every year.

Today, Kamakhya Temple stands as a symbol of faith, spirituality, and the rich cultural heritage of Assam. It is also renowned for its unique Tantric traditions and the famous Ambubachi Mela, celebrated annually with great devotion.
