🪔 Manasha Puja

Goddess of Snakes, Prosperity, Healing and Protection

🌺 About Manasha Puja

Goddess Manasha, also known as Manasa Devi, is worshipped mainly in Assam, West Bengal, Bihar and several parts of eastern India. She is regarded as the Goddess of snakes, fertility, prosperity, healing and protection from snake bites. Devotees worship her with great devotion to seek blessings for good health, family welfare, wealth and protection from all dangers.

📖 Origin & Mythology

According to Hindu mythology, Goddess Manasha is believed to be the daughter of Sage Kashyapa. One of the most famous stories is about Chand Sadagar, a wealthy merchant who refused to worship the Goddess. After many hardships, he accepted her divine power and became one of her greatest devotees. This story is beautifully described in the Manasamangal texts.

🎉 Celebration

The festival is celebrated during July and August. Devotees decorate temples and homes with flowers, offer milk, fruits, sweets and prayers. Traditional music, dance and devotional rituals are performed throughout the festival.
